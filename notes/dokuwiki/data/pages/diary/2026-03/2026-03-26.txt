  * https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betteridge%27s_law_of_headlines - Betteridge's law of headlines
    * "Any headline that ends in a question mark can be answered by the word no." The idea is that if the author was confident that the answer was yes, they would have presented it as an assertion; by presenting it as a question, they are not accountable for whether it is correct or not.

  * https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_rate_fallacy - Base rate fallacy

  * : "The credibility of any statement is inversely proportional to the number and enthusiasm of emojis used to punctuate it."
  * : "If you need an emoji to convey the emotion, the content itself probably isn't doing the work."
  * : "If you have to say you're the king, you're not the king." Strong content carries its own weight without decoration.
